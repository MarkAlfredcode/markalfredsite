How One Committee Turned a Few Big Checks Into 69 Campaign Contributions

A joint fundraising committee lets a single donor's check get split among a member of Congress's own campaign, their state party, the national party — and their leadership PAC, which then hands out pieces of it to campaigns in nearly 30 states. Here's the real path one member's money network took this cycle, built entirely from public FEC filings.

How this was built: Every figure above comes from FEC disclosure filings for the Steil Victory Fund (a joint fundraising committee, FEC ID C00686980) and On Wisconsin PAC, Rep. Bryan Steil's leadership PAC (C00697326), covering the 2025–2026 cycle. Donor totals combine multiple contributions from the same individual; committee-to-committee figures are aggregated from itemized disbursements. About $140,000 in fundraising-consulting and vendor fees along the way isn't shown in the diagram. Steil Victory Fund is On Wisconsin PAC's single largest funding source, at 24.6% of its total receipts. Data pulled from api.open.fec.gov; not affiliated with or endorsed by any of the committees named. This is one documented path money took — not a claim that any single dollar physically traveled this exact route.
